The Regulatory Shift : SEBI's New Guard

For beginners, the most significant change is regulatory. In late 2024, SEBI introduced stricter norms for the Futures & Options segment to curb retail losses.


  • Increased Contract Sizes: As per SEBI mandates, the minimum contract value for index derivatives was raised to ₹15 Lakhs. Consequently, the Nifty 50 lot size stands at 75 units (as of December 2025), requiring significantly higher capital.
  • Discontinued Weekly Expiries: Bank Nifty weekly contracts were discontinued in November 2024. Only monthly contracts remain, reducing the "casino-like" frequency of zero-to-hero trades.

The Implication: The barrier to entry for trading has tripled. For a beginner in 2026, Equity Delivery (buying and holding shares) is not just the safer option; it is the only viable starting point.

The "Core & Satellite" Strategy: Best for 2026


The single best strategy for a beginner in 2026 is the Core & Satellite approach. This institutional-grade strategy balances safety with aggressive growth.


The Core (60-70%): Stability Engine

Your "Core" portfolio ensures you don't underperform the market. It consists of low-cost Index Funds or Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs).


  • Allocation: 60-70% of investable surplus.
  • Instrument: Nifty 50 ETF (Large Cap) and Nifty Next 50 Index Fund (Emerging Bluechips).
  • Why: These passive funds track India's top 100 companies. They eliminate "stock selection risk." If Reliance or HDFC Bank grows, your portfolio grows.

The Satellite (30-40%): Alpha Generators

Your "Satellite" portfolio is where you take calculated risks to beat the market. In 2026, specific sectors are poised for breakout growth due to policy continuity.


  • Allocation: 30-40% of investable surplus.
  • Focus Sectors:

1. Green Energy: With India's target of 500 GW renewable capacity by 2030, companies in solar, wind, and power transmission are entering a maturity phase.

2. Defence Manufacturing: The indigenization push continues to fill order books of PSU and private defence majors.

3. EV Ecosystem: Battery manufacturers and charging infrastructure players are seeing PLI scheme benefits materialize.


Strategy Tip: Don't buy 20 stocks. Pick 5-8 high-conviction stocks in these sectors. If you are unsure, use a "Thematic Mutual Fund" as a proxy.

Step-by-Step Execution Blueprint


Knowing the strategy is half the battle; execution is the other half. Here is your roadmap.


Step 1: The Infrastructure (Demat & KYC)

You cannot invest without a pipeline.

  • Open a Demat Account: Choose a broker that offers robust research (essential for your Satellite portfolio) and stable technology.
  • Complete KYC: As of 2025, the KYC process is entirely digital. You need your PAN, Aadhaar (linked to mobile), and a bank proof.

Step 2: The Funding (SIP Automation)

Discipline beats timing. Set up a Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) for your Core portfolio.

  • Action: Authorize a mandate for the 5th of every month (salary day).
  • Amount: Start with at least 20% of your monthly take-home income.

Step 3: Tax Optimization (Harvesting)

Taxes eat into returns. In FY 2025-26, the tax rules are:

  • STCG (Short Term Capital Gains): 20% (for holdings < 12 months).
  • LTCG (Long Term Capital Gains): 12.5% (for holdings > 12 months).

The Loophole: The first ₹1.25 Lakh of LTCG in a financial year is tax-free.
  • Strategy: Every March, check your unrealized long-term gains. If you have ₹1 Lakh profit, sell the shares and buy them back immediately. You "book" the profit tax-free and reset your buy price higher, reducing future tax liability. This is called Tax Harvesting.

Understanding Risk Factors


Even the best strategy carries risk. Understanding these factors separates investors from gamblers.


Market Volatility

Equity markets are non-linear. A 15% correction is a feature, not a bug.

  • Mitigation: Never invest money you need in the next 3 years. The "Core" portfolio recovers faster than individual stocks.

Sectoral Rotation

Sectors that lead in 2025 might lag in 2027.

  • Mitigation: This is why your Satellite allocation is capped at 40%. If Green Energy stalls, your Nifty 50 Core keeps growing.

Regulatory Risk

SEBI regulations evolve.

  • Mitigation: Stay updated. For instance, knowing that Bank Nifty weekly expiries are discontinued prevents you from searching for non-existent contracts.

FAQ Section


What is the minimum amount to start equity investment in 2026?

You can start with as little as ₹500 per month via SIPs in Index Funds. For direct stocks, you need the price of one share, which can vary from ₹50 to ₹5,000. Most discount brokers have zero account opening fees.


Is F&O trading recommended for beginners in 2026?

No. SEBI's November 2024 regulations increased minimum contract values to over ₹15 Lakhs and discontinued Bank Nifty weekly expiries. The high capital requirement and risk make F&O unsuitable for beginners; Equity Delivery is the safer, wealth-building alternative.


How does the 12.5% LTCG tax affect my returns?

LTCG tax of 12.5% applies only on gains exceeding ₹1.25 Lakh in a financial year. If your annual profit is ₹1 Lakh, you pay zero tax. This exemption makes equity one of the most tax-efficient asset classes for long-term wealth.


Which sectors are best for the 'Satellite' portfolio in 2026?

High-growth sectors aligned with government policy offer the best potential. In 2026, focus on Renewable Energy (500 GW target), Defence Manufacturing (indigenization push), and Electric Mobility. These sectors benefit from PLI schemes and domestic demand.


What is the Nifty 50 lot size for 2026?

As of December 2025, the Nifty 50 lot size is 75 units. However, as per NSE circulars, this will be revised to 65 units for contracts expiring after December 30, 2025 (i.e., January 2026 contracts onwards). Always check the latest NSE specification before trading.
