GIFT Nifty represents a historic shift in India's financial ecosystem. For the average Indian investor, it serves as the primary "gap-up" or "gap-down" signal before the domestic market opens at 9:15 AM.

## Understanding GIFT Nifty: The New Global Benchmark

**What is GIFT Nifty?** simply put, is a futures contract based on the Nifty 50 Index, traded in US Dollars instead of Indian Rupees. It operates at the NSE International Exchange (NSE IX) within the International Financial Services Centre (IFSC) in Gandhinagar.

Think of it as the Nifty 50's global twin. While the domestic Nifty 50 sleeps (markets close at 3:30 PM), GIFT Nifty stays awake, trading for nearly 21 hours a day. This extended window allows it to react to global events—like US Federal Reserve rate decisions or geopolitical tensions—long before the Indian markets open.

### From SGX Nifty to GIFT Nifty
For over two decades, foreign investors traded "SGX Nifty" in Singapore to hedge their Indian portfolios. However, as per the transition finalized in July 2023, all open positions and liquidity shifted to GIFT City. This move, known as the "NSE IX-SGX Connect," ensures that liquidity remains within India's jurisdiction while offering global access.

## GIFT Nifty Trading Hours and Specifications (November 2025)

To use GIFT Nifty effectively, you must understand its schedule. As of November 2025, the exchange operates in two distinct sessions to cover Asian, European, and American market hours.

### Trading Schedule
As per NSE IX circulars effective late 2025, the trading window is divided as follows:

*   **Morning Session (Session 1):** 06:30 AM to 03:40 PM IST
    *   *Significance:* Overlaps with Asian markets (Nikkei, Hang Seng) and European opening bells.
*   **Break:** 03:40 PM to 04:35 PM IST
*   **Evening Session (Session 2):** 04:35 PM to 02:45 AM IST (Next Day)
    *   *Significance:* Captures the entire US trading session (Dow Jones, Nasdaq).

### Contract Specifications
For institutional traders and NRIs, understanding the contract physics is vital. As per NSE IX specifications (November 2025):

| Specification | Details |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Underlying** | Nifty 50 Index |
| **Contract Multiplier** | **$2** per index point |
| **Contract Value** | Index Value × $2 (e.g., 26,000 × $2 = $52,000) |
| **Tick Size** | 0.50 index points ($1 value) |
| **Expiry Day** | **Tuesday** (Weekly contracts)* |
| **Settlement** | Cash Settled in USD |

*Note: Expiry days are subject to exchange revisions. As per recent microstructure enhancements (effective Oct 2025), weekly contracts expire on Tuesdays.*

## Who Can Trade GIFT Nifty?

This is the most critical distinction for our readers. While GIFT Nifty is based on an Indian index, **not every Indian can trade it.**

### 1. Resident Indian Retail Investors (The Restriction)
If you are a resident Indian living in Mumbai, Delhi, or Bangalore, **you generally cannot trade GIFT Nifty derivatives.**

Why? Trading requires remitting funds outside India. While the Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS) allows you to send up to $250,000 annually abroad, the RBI explicitly **prohibits** using LRS funds for "margin trading" or "leveraged financial products" like futures and options. Since GIFT Nifty is a leveraged derivative, it falls under this restriction.

*   **Can you buy stocks in GIFT City?** Yes (via permissible channels).
*   **Can you trade GIFT Nifty Futures?** No (typically restricted for retail speculation).

### 2. Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) and Foreign Investors
For NRIs and Foreign Portfolio Investors (FPIs), GIFT Nifty is a goldmine. They can trade freely without the Currency Risk of the Indian Rupee (INR), as the contract is in USD.

**Tax Advantage:** As per Section 10(4E) of the Income Tax Act (verified for FY 2025-26), income arising from the transfer of non-deliverable forward contracts or derivatives by a non-resident on a recognized IFSC exchange is **100% tax-exempt**.

## FAQs on What is GIFT Nifty?

### What is the difference between Nifty 50 and GIFT Nifty?
Nifty 50 is the domestic index traded in Indian Rupees (INR) on the NSE from 9:15 AM to 3:30 PM. GIFT Nifty is a dollar-denominated (USD) derivative of the Nifty 50 traded on NSE IX in GIFT City for 21 hours daily.

### Can I trade GIFT Nifty in Zerodha or Upstox?
No, regular domestic trading accounts with Indian brokers cannot access GIFT Nifty. It requires a specific trading account with a broker registered at NSE IX in GIFT City. Resident Indians are generally restricted from trading these derivatives under LRS rules.

### What are the trading timings for GIFT Nifty in 2025?
As of November 2025, GIFT Nifty operates in two sessions: Session 1 from 06:30 AM to 03:40 PM IST, and Session 2 from 04:35 PM to 02:45 AM IST (next day). This schedule aligns with Asian, European, and US market hours.

### Is GIFT Nifty tax-free for Indian investors?
For resident Indians, trading is restricted, so tax questions are moot. For NRIs and Foreign Portfolio Investors (FPIs), profits from derivatives trading on NSE IX are 100% tax-exempt under Section 10(4E) of the Income Tax Act.

### Why did SGX Nifty move to GIFT City?
The move was driven by the Indian government's initiative to bring offshore trading liquidity back to India. The "NSE IX-SGX Connect" allows global investors to trade Nifty derivatives within India's regulatory framework (IFSCA) while retaining the ease of access they had in Singapore.

**Important Notes:**
*   *Expiry days and contract specifications are subject to periodic revision by NSE IX. Always verify the latest circulars on the official NSE IX website before trading.*
*   *LRS rules regarding margin trading are strict. Resident investors should consult a chartered accountant before attempting any overseas derivative transactions.*
