## Summary
This article covers the fundamentals of annuities, a critical retirement tool that converts a lump sum into a guaranteed lifetime income. We analyze the different types available in India (Immediate vs. Deferred), the impact of the **new 0% GST rule** effective September 2025, and how they compare to alternatives like SCSS (8.2%). The guide also explains the mandatory 40% annuity rule for NPS subscribers and provides real-world calculation examples for FY 2025-26.

Think of an annuity as a "reverse EMI." Instead of you paying the bank monthly to clear a loan, an insurance company pays you a fixed monthly sum for life in exchange for a one-time lump sum payment. It is the only financial product in India that guarantees income for as long as you live, eliminating the risk of outliving your savings. While returns (typically 6-7%) are lower than equity markets, the certainty they offer makes them a cornerstone of retirement planning for risk-averse investors.

## Understanding the Basics: What Is Annuity?

At its core, an annuity is a contract between you and a life insurance company. You pay a premium (either a lump sum or a series of payments), and in return, the insurer provides regular periodic payments for a specified period or for life.

### The Mechanism
1.  **Accumulation Phase:** You invest money. This can be a gradual process (like in a Deferred Annuity) or a one-time transfer (like in an Immediate Annuity).
2.  **Vesting Age:** The age at which your payouts begin.
3.  **Payout Phase:** The insurer pays you monthly, quarterly, or annually. This income is treated as salary or income from other sources and is taxed accordingly.

### Why Consider It?
The biggest risk in retirement isn't market volatility; it's **longevity risk**—the possibility that you will live longer than your savings last. Annuities transfer this risk to the insurance company.

## Types of Annuities in India

Broadly, annuities fall into two categories based on *when* the income starts.

### 1. Immediate Annuity
This is the most common form for retirees. You pay a lump sum (say, ₹50 Lakh), and the pension starts the very next month.
*   **Best for:** Someone retiring today who has a corpus (like EPF or Gratuity) and needs immediate monthly cash flow.
*   **Lock-in:** Usually permanent; you cannot withdraw the principal once invested (except in specific critical illness clauses in some policies).

### 2. Deferred Annuity
You invest today, but the payouts start after a few years (deferment period). During this gap, your money grows.
*   **Best for:** A 50-year-old who has a surplus now but wants income to start at age 60.
*   **Benefit:** The corpus accumulates interest during the deferment period, potentially leading to higher payouts later.

### Payout Options Explained
Within these categories, you can choose *how* you get paid. This choice affects your return rate.

| Option | How It Works | Return Rate (Approx.) | Suitability |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Life Annuity** | Pays as long as you live. Stops upon death. No money returned to family. | Highest (~7-7.5%) | Singles or those with no dependents. |
| **Life Annuity with Return of Purchase Price (ROP)** | Pays for life. Upon death, the initial investment is returned to the nominee. | Lower (~6-6.5%) | Those who want to leave a legacy. |
| **Joint Life Annuity** | Pays you for life, then pays your spouse for life. | Moderate | Couples ensuring spouse security. |

**Important Note:** As per current market rates (November 2025), opting for "Return of Purchase Price" reduces your monthly payout by roughly 15-20% compared to a plain Life Annuity because the insurer has to preserve your capital.

## The September 2025 GST Update: A Game Changer

For years, a major criticism of annuities was the 1.8% GST levied on the purchase price. If you bought an annuity for ₹1 Crore, you effectively paid ₹1.8 Lakh in tax just to enter the product.

**As of September 22, 2025, the GST Council has reduced the GST on individual annuity plans to 0%.**

*   **Old Rule:** ₹1 Cr investment = ₹1.8 Lakh GST = Total cost ₹1.018 Cr.
*   **New Rule (Nov 2025):** ₹1 Cr investment = ₹0 GST = Total cost ₹1 Cr.

This immediate saving increases your effective yield. Note that this exemption applies to **individual** policies; group annuity schemes (often used by employers) may still attract GST.

## NPS and Annuities: The Mandatory Link

For millions of corporate and government employees, understanding annuities is mandatory, not optional, due to the National Pension System (NPS).

### The 40% Rule
As per PFRDA regulations active in November 2025:
*   At age 60, you can withdraw **60%** of your NPS corpus tax-free.
*   You **MUST** use the remaining **40%** to purchase an annuity from an impaneled insurer (like LIC, SBI Life, HDFC Life, etc.).

*Example:* If your NPS corpus is ₹2 Crore:
*   ₹1.2 Crore: Withdrawn as lump sum (Tax-Free).
*   ₹80 Lakh: Compulsorily locked into an annuity.

### The "Proposed" 20% Change
*Note:* You might read news about this limit dropping to 20%. As of November 2025, this is a **proposal** by PFRDA to offer more flexibility. Until officially notified, the 40% rule remains the law. Always verify the latest circular before maturity.

## Returns Calculation: Real Numbers (FY 2025-26)

Let’s calculate what a ₹50 Lakh investment yields for a 60-year-old in November 2025.

*Assumption: Immediate Annuity, Monthly Payout Mode.*

| Plan Type | Interest Rate (Approx.) | Monthly Income | Annual Income |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Life Annuity (No ROP)** | 7.2% | ₹30,000 | ₹3.60 Lakh |
| **Life Annuity with ROP** | 6.0% | ₹25,000 | ₹3.00 Lakh |
| **Joint Life with ROP** | 5.8% | ₹24,166 | ₹2.90 Lakh |

*Data Source: General industry average for private and public insurers, Nov 2025. Rates vary by age and provider.*

**Analysis:**
Notice the trade-off. If you insist on getting your capital back (ROP), you lose about ₹5,000/month in income. If you don't have heirs, the "No ROP" option maximizes your lifestyle today.

## Annuity vs. SCSS vs. SWP

How does an annuity stack up against other income generators like the Senior Citizen Savings Scheme (SCSS) or Mutual Fund SWP?

| Feature | Annuity (Life with ROP) | SCSS (Senior Citizen Savings Scheme) | Mutual Fund SWP (Conservative Hybrid) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Returns (Nov 2025)** | ~6.0% Fixed for Life | **8.2%** (Fixed for 5 years) | Market Linked (Expect 8-9%) |
| **Tenure** | Lifetime | 5 Years (Extendable by 3) | Flexible |
| **Liquidity** | Zero (Locked) | High (Penalty applies) | High (Anytime) |
| **Taxation** | Taxed at Slab Rate | Taxed at Slab Rate | Efficient (LTCG benefits) |
| **Risk** | Zero (Guaranteed) | Zero (Govt Backed) | Low-Moderate (Market Risk) |

**Verdict:**
SCSS currently beats Annuity on rates (8.2% vs 6%). Most advisors recommend maxing out the SCSS limit (₹30 Lakh per individual) *before* buying an annuity, unless you specifically need the "lifetime guarantee" that SCSS (which matures in 5 years) cannot provide.

## Taxation of Annuities

There is a misconception that annuity income is tax-free. It is **not**.

1.  **Income Tax:** The monthly pension you receive is added to your total income and taxed as per your slab (Old or New Regime).
    *   *Example:* If you receive ₹3 Lakh annuity and have ₹10 Lakh other income, you are taxed on ₹13 Lakh.
2.  **No Standard Deduction:** Unlike salary, standard deduction is generally not available on annuity income purchased voluntarily. However, annuities from **NPS** or employer superannuation funds are treated as "Salary" and are eligible for the ₹50,000 (or ₹75,000 under new regime updates) standard deduction.

## Who Should Buy an Annuity?

Annuities are not for wealth creation; they are for **wealth consumption**.

**Buy if:**
*   You are risk-averse and worry about market crashes.
*   You fear outliving your savings (longevity risk).
*   You lack the discipline to manage a large corpus (preventing you from spending it all too fast).
*   You want a "shut it and forget it" income stream.

**Avoid if:**
*   You have a small corpus (under ₹30 Lakh) – prioritize SCSS first.
*   You have high liquidity needs (medical, family).
*   You are already in a high tax bracket (annuity adds to taxable income inefficiently).

## Key Takeaways

*   **0% GST Benefit:** Buying an annuity is cheaper now than in early 2025 due to the GST waiver on individual plans.
*   **Lifetime Guarantee:** It is the only product that pays you even if you live to 100, protecting against longevity risk.
*   **NPS Mandate:** If you have an NPS account, 40% of your maturity corpus will automatically go into an annuity.
*   **ROP Trade-off:** Choosing "Return of Purchase Price" lowers your monthly income by ~15-20%. Only choose it if leaving a legacy is essential.
*   **Tax Trap:** Remember that annuity income is fully taxable. Factor this into your net income calculations.
*   **SCSS First:** With SCSS offering 8.2% (Nov 2025), max out that ₹30 Lakh limit before locking money into a 6% annuity.

**Month 1: Assessment**
*   Calculate your monthly non-negotiable expenses (food, bills, medicine).
*   Check your total retirement corpus (EPF, PPF, NPS, MFs).
*   Identify the "gap" between your guaranteed income (like rental or interest) and your expenses.

**Month 2: Product Selection**
*   If you are over 60, visit your bank or post office to open an SCSS account for the first ₹30 Lakh of your corpus (Current Rate: 8.2%).

*   Purchase the annuity. Ensure you check for "Online Purchase Incentives" (often 1-2% higher rates).

## Conclusion

Annuities are the safety net of retirement planning. They won't make you rich, but they ensure you never become poor. With the recent removal of GST and stable rates of 6-7%, they remain a vital tool for the conservative investor. However, they should rarely be your *only* investment. A balanced retirement portfolio typically combines SCSS for high returns, Annuities for lifetime safety, and Mutual Funds for inflation protection.

## FAQs on Annuities

### What happens to my annuity money if I die?
It depends on the option chosen. In a standard "Life Annuity," payouts stop, and money is **not** returned. In "Return of Purchase Price" (ROP) variants, the initial invested amount is returned to your nominee. ROP plans offer lower monthly payouts to cover this benefit.

### Is annuity income tax-free in India?
No. Annuity income is treated as "Income from Other Sources" (or Salary for NPS annuities) and is taxed at your applicable slab rate. For example, if you are in the 30% bracket, a 6% annuity return effectively becomes ~4.2% post-tax.

### Can I surrender my annuity policy if I need money?
Generally, no. Annuities are designed to be illiquid to ensure lifetime income. Some modern policies allow surrender only in specific critical illness scenarios (like cancer or kidney failure), often subject to surrender charges. Do not invest emergency funds here.

### How does the 0% GST rule affect me?
Effective September 22, 2025, individual annuity purchases attract 0% GST (down from 1.8%). This means if you invest ₹10 Lakh, the entire amount goes towards generating income, slightly increasing your effective payout compared to previous years.

### Which is better: SCSS or Annuity?
For the first ₹30 Lakh, SCSS is superior due to higher rates (8.2% as of Nov 2025) and shorter lock-in (5 years). Annuities are better for amounts exceeding the SCSS limit or for investors who want a guaranteed income for life, not just 5 years.
