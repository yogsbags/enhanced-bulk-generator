## How Does Scalping Trading Work

Scalping works on the principle of \"volume over value.\" Instead of waiting for a stock to move 5% in a day, a scalper aims for 0.1% to 0.5% moves multiple times a session. The goal is to accumulate small wins that add up to a substantial daily profit.

### Execution Mechanics
In the Indian context, scalpers typically target the **Nifty 50** or **Sensex** indices due to their high liquidity. Since SEBI and NSE discontinued weekly contracts for Bank Nifty and FinNifty (leaving only monthly contracts as of November 2025), liquidity has consolidated in Nifty 50 weekly options.

*   **Timeframe:** Trades last from a few seconds to 5 minutes. A position held longer than 15 minutes usually transitions into a standard intraday trade.
*   **Profit Targets:** Scalpers might target 5-10 points on Nifty futures or ₹2-3 on a stock like Reliance. The focus is on the **win rate**—you need to be right 60-70% of the time to cover costs.
*   **Volume Requirements:** You need high volumes to enter and exit without slippage. Slippage (the difference between your intended price and execution price) is the scalper's enemy.

### Platform Requirements
Speed is non-negotiable. You cannot scalp effectively using a basic mobile app on a 4G connection. Professional scalpers use:
*   **Direct Market Access (DMA)** or high-speed broker terminals.
*   **One-click trading** features to execute orders instantly.
*   **Low-latency internet** to ensure charts and order books update in real-time.

### Example: Scalping Nifty 50 Futures
Imagine Nifty 50 is trading at 24,500. A scalper sees a sudden spike in buying volume at 24,500.50.
1.  **Entry:** Buy 1 lot (75 units) at 24,501.
2.  **Exit:** Sell at 24,506 within 45 seconds.
3.  **Gross Profit:** 5 points × 75 units = ₹375.
4.  **Repeat:** Doing this 20 times a day generates ₹7,500 gross profit (before taxes and charges).

## How Do Scalpers Analyse the Market Before Scalping

Successful scalping is 90% preparation and 10% execution. You cannot react to price; you must anticipate it. Scalpers rely heavily on technical analysis and real-time data flow.

### Order Book Analysis (Level 2 Data)
Standard charts show past price. The **Order Book (Market Depth)** shows future intent. Scalpers look for:
*   **Bid-Ask Imbalances:** A sudden stack of buy orders (bids) significantly outweighing sell orders (asks) suggests an immediate price tick upward.
*   **Large Orders:** Institutional \"iceberg\" orders that absorb selling pressure often act as short-term support levels.

### Volume and Liquidity Assessment
You need stocks with tight **bid-ask spreads**. If a stock is trading at ₹1000 with a bid of ₹999.50 and ask of ₹1000.50, the ₹1 spread is too wide for scalping. You want a spread of ₹0.05 or ₹0.10. High liquidity ensures you can exit a losing trade instantly.

### Technical Indicators for Speed
Scalpers use fast-reacting indicators on 1-minute or tick charts:
*   **VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price):** Acts as a dynamic support/resistance line for intraday moves.
*   **Stochastic Oscillator:** Helps identify overbought/oversold conditions in ultra-short timeframes.
*   **Exponential Moving Averages (EMA):** A 9-period or 20-period EMA often guides the immediate trend.

### Time-of-Day Considerations
*   **Opening Bell (9:15 AM - 10:00 AM):** Highest volatility. Prices move fast, offering maximum opportunity but highest risk.
*   **Closing Session (2:30 PM - 3:30 PM):** Intraday square-offs create rapid price swings.
*   **Mid-Day (11:00 AM - 1:00 PM):** Often choppy or sideways; most scalpers avoid this zone.

## Differences Between Day Trading and Scalping Trading

While both are intraday strategies, they differ fundamentally in pace and risk profile. Here is a comparison based on current market dynamics.

| Feature | Scalping Trading | Day Trading (Intraday) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Holding Period** | Seconds to Minutes | Minutes to Hours |
| **Primary Goal** | Capture tiny price changes (ticks) | Capture larger intraday trends |
| **Trades Per Day** | High (20 to 100+) | Low to Medium (1 to 5) |
| **Profit Target** | Small (0.1% - 0.2% per trade) | Medium (1% - 3% per trade) |
| **Capital Requirement** | High (needs margin for multiple lots) | Moderate |
| **Stress Level** | Extremely High (Constant focus) | Moderate |
| **Risk Per Trade** | Very Low (tight stop-losses) | Moderate (wider stop-losses) |
| **Analysis Type** | Order Flow, Level 2 Data, Tick Charts | Chart Patterns, 15-min/Hourly Candles |
| **Cost Sensitivity** | High (Commissions eat into profits) | Moderate |

## Advantages and Disadvantages of Scalping Trading

Scalping is not for everyone. It requires a specific temperament and understanding of the cost structure, especially with the FY 2025-26 tax changes.

### Advantages
*   **Limited Overnight Risk:** You close all positions before the market closes. You are immune to overnight global news or gap-down openings.
*   **Quick Profit Realization:** You don't need to wait hours for a target. Profits are booked instantly.
*   **Market Neutrality:** You can scalp in both rising (bull) and falling (bear) markets effectively.
*   **High Opportunity Frequency:** Even in a range-bound market where day traders struggle, scalpers can find small oscillations to exploit.
*   **Compound Effect:** Small consistent gains of ₹500-₹1000 can compound into a significant monthly income if losses are controlled.

### Disadvantages
*   **High Transaction Costs:** This is the biggest hurdle. As per the **Union Budget 2024**, STT on options selling increased to **0.1%** and futures to **0.02%**. For a scalper doing 50 trades, these costs (plus brokerage, exchange charges, and GST) can consume 30-40% of gross profits.
*   **Mental Fatigue:** Staring at a screen for hours requires intense concentration. One lapse in focus can lead to a large loss.
*   **Execution Risk:** A 2-second internet lag or a \"fat finger\" error can wipe out the profits of 10 successful trades.
*   **Tax Implications:** Profits are treated as **Speculative (Intraday Equity)** or **Non-Speculative (F&O) Business Income**. They are taxed at your slab rate (up to 30% + cess), unlike the 20% STCG rate for delivery trades.

## How to Choose Stocks for Scalping

Not every stock is suitable for scalping. You need stocks that move enough to give profit but have enough liquidity to let you out.

### Selection Criteria
1.  **High Liquidity:** Look for stocks with high average daily volume. This ensures tight bid-ask spreads.
2.  **Beta (Volatility):** You need stocks with a Beta > 1.0, meaning they move more than the market. Dead stocks don't generate scalping signals.
3.  **Low Impact Cost:** This is a measure of liquidity. Lower impact cost means your large order won't move the price against you.
4.  **Derivatives Presence:** Stocks in the F&O segment typically have better liquidity and no circuit limits (at the strike level) compared to cash-only stocks.

### Best Candidates for Scalping (November 2025)
*   **Indices:** **Nifty 50** (Tuesday Expiry) and **Sensex** (Thursday Expiry) are the gold standards.
*   **Banking Giants:** **HDFC Bank**, **ICICI Bank**, **State Bank of India (SBI)**. These have massive liquidity.
*   **High-Volume Movers:** **Reliance Industries**, **Tata Steel**, **Infosys**. These stocks have deep order books, allowing entry/exit of large quantities without slippage.

## Scalping Trading Strategies

Here are three actionable strategies used by Indian scalpers.

### 1. Momentum Scalping (The Breakout)
This strategy chases a stock that is already moving. You enter when a stock breaks a key intraday resistance level with high volume.
*   **Setup:** Identify a \"flag\" pattern on a 1-minute chart.
*   **Trigger:** Buy immediately when price crosses the flag high.
*   **Exit:** Sell as soon as momentum stalls (usually 30-60 seconds).
*   **Example:** Nifty breaks 24,600. You buy a Call option. As it hits 24,605, you exit. You don't wait for 24,650.

### 2. Range Trading Scalping (The Mean Reversion)
Used when the market is sideways. You buy at support and sell at resistance.
*   **Setup:** Stock is bouncing between ₹500 and ₹505.
*   **Trigger:** Buy at ₹500.50 with a stop loss at ₹499.50.
*   **Exit:** Sell at ₹504.00. Do not wait for the breakout.
*   **Tool:** Bollinger Bands are excellent for this. Buy at the lower band, sell at the middle band.

### 3. Order Flow Scalping
This relies purely on the **Level 2 (Market Depth)** window, ignoring charts.
*   **Setup:** You see a huge \"buy wall\" (e.g., 50,000 shares) at ₹100.00.
*   **Action:** You place a buy order at ₹100.05, front-running the wall.
*   **Logic:** The large order will act as a floor. If price moves up to ₹100.50, you exit. If the buy wall starts disappearing (cancelled), you exit immediately.

## Key Takeaways

*   **Speed is Currency:** Scalping is about capturing small moves (ticks) frequently. It requires split-second decision-making.
*   **Know Your Costs:** With STT at 0.1% on options premium and 0.02% on futures, high-volume scalping can be expensive. Calculate your break-even points carefully.
*   **Focus on Nifty & Sensex:** With Bank Nifty weekly contracts discontinued, liquidity has shifted. Adjust your strategy to Nifty (Tue expiry) and Sensex (Thu expiry).
*   **Strict Stop-Losses:** A scalper cannot afford to become an investor. If a trade goes wrong by 2-3 ticks, cut it. Hope is not a strategy.
*   **Regulatory Awareness:** Stay updated on SEBI margin rules and expiry schedules, as these change the structural landscape of intraday trading.
*   **Tax Compliance:** Remember that scalping profits are fully taxable business income. Maintain proper books for audit if turnover exceeds limits.

## FAQs on Scalping Trading

### What is scalping trading in the Indian stock market?
Scalping is an intraday strategy where traders execute multiple trades to capture small price changes. In India, it typically involves Nifty/Sensex futures or options, holding positions for seconds to minutes to accumulate small profits that compound over the day.

### Is scalping trading profitable in India with high STT?
Yes, but it requires a high win rate. With STT on options selling at 0.1% (as of FY 2025-26), your profit per trade must be large enough to cover these costs. Scalpers often switch to Futures (0.02% STT) to manage expenses better.

### Is scalping trading legal in India?
Yes, scalping is 100% legal. It is treated as intraday or F&O trading. However, you must comply with SEBI's margin rules (100% upfront margin) and ensure you are not engaging in manipulative practices like spoofing or circular trading.

### Is scalping better than day trading?
It depends on your personality. Scalping requires intense focus and quick reflexes, suitable for those who want no overnight risk. Day trading is slower, targeting larger moves. Scalping has higher transaction costs but offers more frequent opportunities.

### What is the best timeframe for scalping?
Most scalpers use **1-minute** or **3-minute** charts. Some advanced traders use **tick charts** or **range bars** to filter out time-based noise and focus purely on price movement and volume action.

**Important Notes:**
*   *As per NSE circular dated November 2025, Nifty 50 lot size is 75 units and Bank Nifty lot size is 35 units. These are scheduled to reduce to 65 and 30 units respectively in January 2026.*
*   *Bank Nifty weekly expiry contracts were discontinued in late 2024; currently, only monthly contracts are available for Bank Nifty.*
