## What is Sensex?

The **Sensex** (Sensitivity Index) is the benchmark index of the Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE). Launched on January 1, 1986, it is the oldest stock index in India. It tracks the performance of **30 largest and most actively traded stocks** listed on the BSE, representing various industrial sectors of the Indian economy.

While the index was launched in 1986, it uses **1978-79 as the base year** with a base value of 100. This means if the Sensex is trading at roughly 84,600 today (November 2025), it represents a growth of over 840 times from its base value over four decades.

## How is Sensex Calculated?

The Sensex uses the **Free-float Market Capitalization** method. This methodology ensures that the index reflects the market value of shares actually available for trading, excluding shares locked in by promoters, governments, or strategic investors.

### The Calculation Formula

The formula might look complex, but the concept is straightforward:

**Sensex = (Total Free-float Market Cap of 30 Companies / Base Market Cap) × Base Index Value (100)**

Here is how it works in practice:
1.  **Market Cap**: Calculate the total value of a company (Price × Total Shares).
2.  **Free-float Factor**: Determine what percentage of shares are available to the public (e.g., if Promoters hold 60%, free float is 40% or 0.40).
3.  **Weightage**: Companies with higher free-float market cap get a higher weightage in the index. This is why **HDFC Bank** often has a higher weightage than companies with larger total market caps but lower free float.

## How to Invest in Sensex

You cannot buy the "Sensex" directly like a stock. However, you can invest in products that mimic its performance. Here are the three most effective ways for Indian investors in FY 2025-26.

### 1. Sensex Index Funds
These are mutual funds that invest in the same 30 companies in the exact same proportion as the index.
*   **Pros**: Low expense ratios (typically 0.10% - 0.30%), automatic diversification, no fund manager bias.
*   **Cons**: You cannot beat the market; you only match it.
*   **Best For**: SIP investors looking for long-term wealth creation.

### 2. Sensex ETFs (Exchange Traded Funds)
ETFs are like mutual funds that trade on the stock exchange. You need a Demat account to buy them.
*   **Pros**: Real-time trading, lower costs than index funds.
*   **Cons**: You must pay brokerage and demat charges; liquidity can be an issue for smaller ETFs.
*   **Best For**: Investors who want to buy/sell during market hours.

### 3. Sensex Futures & Options (F&O)
For advanced traders, Sensex derivatives allow hedging and speculation.
*   **Lot Size**: As per BSE specifications (November 2025), the lot size is **20 units**.
*   **Expiry**: Weekly contracts expire every **Thursday** (subject to exchange holidays). *Note: The expiry day was revised to Thursday effective September 2025.*
*   **Risk**: High. This is for trading, not investing.

## Taxation for Sensex Investors (FY 2025-26)

Profits from Sensex funds or ETFs are taxed under the capital gains head. As per the Union Budget 2025, the rates are:

*   **Short Term Capital Gains (STCG)**: If sold within 12 months, profits are taxed at **20%**.
*   **Long Term Capital Gains (LTCG)**: If held for more than 12 months, profits above ₹1.25 lakh in a financial year are taxed at **12.5%**.

*Example*: If you earn ₹2 lakh profit after 3 years, the first ₹1.25 lakh is tax-free. The remaining ₹75,000 is taxed at 12.5%.

## FAQs on Sensex

### What is the full form of Sensex?
Sensex stands for **Stock Exchange Sensitive Index**. It was coined by market analyst Deepak Mohoni in 1989. It reflects the sensitivity of the stock market to domestic and global economic events through the movement of 30 major stocks.

### Can I invest ₹500 in Sensex?
Yes, you can. Through **Sensex Index Funds**, you can start a Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) with as little as ₹500 per month. If buying ETFs, you need to buy at least one unit, which costs roughly 1/10th or 1/100th of the index value depending on the fund structure.

### How often do Sensex stocks change?
The BSE reconstructs the index **semi-annually**, typically in June and December. Companies that fail to meet the free-float market cap or liquidity criteria are replaced by better-performing candidates to maintain the index's quality.

### What is the difference between Sensex and Bankex?
Sensex is a diversified index tracking 30 companies across all sectors (Tech, Auto, Pharma, Finance). **Bankex** is a sectoral index that tracks only the banking stocks listed on the BSE. Bankex is much more volatile and focused solely on the financial sector.

### Is Sensex expiry on Tuesday or Thursday?
As of November 2025, the weekly expiry day for Sensex F&O contracts is **Thursday**. It was briefly shifted to Tuesday in early 2025 but was revised to Thursday effective September 2025 to balance market volumes across exchanges.

**Important Notes:**
*   *F&O lot sizes and expiry days are subject to periodic revision by BSE/SEBI. Always verify the latest contract specifications on the BSE website before trading.*
*   *Tax rates mentioned are as per Finance Act 2025 and applicable for FY 2025-26.*
