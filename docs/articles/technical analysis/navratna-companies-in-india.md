## What is a Navratna Company?

A **Navratna Company** is a Central Public Sector Enterprise (CPSE) that has been granted enhanced financial and operational freedom by the Department of Public Enterprises. The term \"Navratna\" translates to \"Nine Gems,\" originally referring to the nine distinguished courtiers in the court of Emperor Akbar. In the modern corporate context, it refers to PSUs that have demonstrated strong financial performance and efficiency.

The primary advantage of this status is **autonomy**. Unlike standard PSUs that need government nods for most big-ticket decisions, a Navratna company can invest up to **₹1,000 crore** or **15% of its net worth** (whichever is lower) in a single project without seeking prior approval from the central government. This agility allows them to form joint ventures, establish subsidiaries in India or abroad, and respond faster to market dynamics.

## What Is the Eligibility Criteria to Become a Navratna Company?

Achieving Navratna status is not easy. A PSU must first be a **Miniratna Category I** company and listed in Schedule 'A' of CPSEs. Additionally, it must have obtained an 'Excellent' or 'Very Good' MOU rating in three of the last five years.

The decisive factor is a Composite Score of **60 or above out of 100** based on six specific performance parameters:

1.  **Net Profit to Net Worth**: Measures profitability relative to shareholder equity.
2.  **Manpower Cost to Total Cost of Production**: Efficiency of workforce utilization.
3.  **PBDIT to Capital Employed**: Profit before depreciation, interest, and taxes vs. capital invested.
4.  **PBDIT to Turnover**: Operating profit margin.
5.  **Earnings Per Share (EPS)**: Profit allocated to each share.
6.  **Inter-Sectoral Performance**: Comparison with peers in the same sector.

## Benefits of Being a Navratna Company in India

The upgrade from Miniratna to Navratna is a game-changer for a PSU's operational freedom.

- **Financial Autonomy**: The Board can approve investments up to **₹1,000 crore** or **15% of net worth** (whichever is lower) for a single project. This eliminates the red tape of seeking ministry approval for every mid-sized project.
- **Global Footprint**: Navratna companies can form joint ventures (JVs), strategic alliances, and establish subsidiaries in India and abroad. For example, OVL (ONGC Videsh) acquires oil assets globally using this freedom.
- **HR Independence**: They have greater powers in organizational restructuring, creation of posts below Board level, and human resource management, allowing them to attract better talent.
- **Ceiling on Investment**: While they have project-level autonomy, the total investment in all joint ventures and subsidiaries combined is capped at **30% of their net worth**.

## Should You Invest in Navratna Companies?

Investing in Navratna companies can be a double-edged sword. You get the safety of government backing, but you also face regulatory risks. Here is the trade-off for FY 2025-26:

### The Pros

- **High Dividend Yields**: PSUs are known for generous dividends. Coal India (Maharatna) and NMDC (Navratna) often offer yields of 4-6%, far beating fixed deposit rates.
- **Valuation Comfort**: Despite the recent rally, many Navratna stocks trade at lower P/E multiples compared to their private sector peers.
- **Sovereign Backing**: The implicit guarantee of the Government of India provides a safety net against bankruptcy.

### The Cons

- **Government Interference**: Decisions can sometimes be driven by social welfare or political goals rather than pure profitability (e.g., price caps on fertilizers or power).
- **OFS Risk**: The government frequently sells stakes through Offer for Sale (OFS) to meet divestment targets, which can temporarily suppress stock prices.
- **Efficiency Lags**: While improving, some PSUs still struggle with slower decision-making compared to agile private competitors.

**Tax Implication Note:** As per Budget 2024-25, dividends are taxed at your slab rate. If you sell these stocks for a profit, **STCG is 20%** (for holdings < 12 months) and **LTCG is 12.5%** (for holdings > 12 months) on gains exceeding ₹1.25 lakh.

## FAQ's on Navratna Companies in India

### How are Navratna Companies regulated and monitored by the government?

Navratna companies are monitored by the Department of Public Enterprises (DPE) under the Ministry of Finance. Their performance is reviewed annually against the Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) targets set with their administrative ministries. Failure to maintain performance can lead to a downgrade in status.

### How many Navratna companies are there in India?

As of November 2025, there are **26 Navratna companies** in India. The list was expanded in 2024 and 2025 with the inclusion of companies like IRCTC, IRFC, NHPC, SJVN, and Mazagon Dock Shipbuilders.

### Who grants Navratna status to companies?

The status is granted by the **Department of Public Enterprises (DPE)**, Ministry of Finance, Government of India. The proposal is usually initiated by the administrative ministry of the respective PSU and approved based on the eligibility criteria.

### What is the significance of Navratna companies in India’s economy?

Navratna companies are pivotal to India's core sectors like infrastructure, power, and defense. They contribute significantly to the exchequer through dividends and taxes, drive capital expenditure (Capex), and implement critical national projects like railway expansion and renewable energy targets.

### Can a Navratna company become a Maharatna?

## Yes, a Navratna company can be upgraded to Maharatna status if it meets higher criteria, such as an average annual net profit of over ₹5,000 crore and an average annual turnover of ₹25,000 crore for three years. Recent upgrades include **Oil India (2023)** and **HAL (2024)**.
