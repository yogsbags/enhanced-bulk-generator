The New Rules of Wealth: Real Estate as the Growth Engine

Real estate has long been the cornerstone of Indian wealth, but the rules of engagement changed dramatically in FY 2025-26. The focus has shifted from mere capital appreciation to tax-efficient structuring, especially given the bifurcation between the Old and New Tax Regimes.

1. Taxation Shifts: The 12.5% vs. 20% Dilemma

For property transactions in December 2025, the capital gains tax landscape is nuanced. As per the Finance Act 2024 (effective July 23, 2024), the Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG) tax rate for real estate has been standardized to 12.5% without indexation for new purchases. However, for properties acquired before this date, a grandfathering clause offers a crucial choice.

| Property Purchase Date | Tax Regime Option 1 | Tax Regime Option 2 | Recommendation |

| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |

| Before July 23, 2024 | 12.5% (No Indexation) | 20% (With Indexation) | Calculate both; Indexation often lowers tax for older properties. |

| After July 23, 2024 | 12.5% (No Indexation) | Not Applicable | Focus on high-growth corridors to beat inflation. |

Data Source: Finance Act 2024/2025 provisions.

2. The New Regime "Loss Set-Off" Trap

A critical, often overlooked change in FY 2025-26 concerns the New Tax Regime. Historically, investors used the loss from house property (where home loan interest exceeds rental income) to set off up to ₹2 lakh against their salary income, reducing their total tax liability.

The Change: As of December 2025, under the New Tax Regime, inter-head set-off of house property loss is NOT allowed. You can deduct interest only up to the extent of rental income (bringing net rental income to zero), but you cannot carry a loss over to your salary head. Impact: High-income earners (30% slab) losing this ₹2 lakh set-off pay approximately ₹60,000 more in taxes annually. Investors relying on this benefit must stick to the Old Tax Regime or restructure their debt.

Gold: The Strategic Hedge and ETF Comeback

Gold is no longer just jewelry; it is a financial instrument for wealth optimization. The discontinuation of new Sovereign Gold Bond (SGB) tranches in 2025 has pushed investors toward the secondary market and ETFs, which have become significantly more attractive due to tax rationalization.

1. The ETF Tax Advantage (12.5% LTCG)

Prior to FY 2025, Gold ETFs were taxed at slab rates (marginal tax rate), making them inefficient for high earners. The Finance Act 2024/2025 reversed this.

Current Status (December 2025):
  • Holding Period for LTCG: Reduced to 12 months for listed Gold ETFs.
  • LTCG Tax Rate: 12.5% (without indexation).

This change makes Gold ETFs a superior liquidity tool compared to physical gold (which requires 24 months for LTCG) and Gold Fund of Funds (24 months for LTCG). You can now tactically enter and exit gold positions with a one-year view and pay a flat 12.5% tax on gains.

2. SGB Secondary Market Opportunities

With the primary issuance market paused, the secondary market (NSE/BSE) is the only route to acquire SGBs.

  • Taxation on Transfer: If you buy SGBs on the exchange and sell them before maturity, you pay 12.5% LTCG (if held >12 months). The "tax-free" benefit applies only if held to maturity or redeemed via RBI window.
  • Yield: Many SGBs trade at a premium, but the 2.5% annual interest (on face value) provides a yield kicker that physical gold lacks.

The "Barbell Strategy": Optimizing the Mix

To achieve true wealth optimization, we recommend a "Barbell Strategy" that pairs the high-leverage growth potential of real estate with the liquid stability of gold.

Why This Works

Real estate is illiquid but allows you to control a large asset base with a small down payment (leverage). Gold is unleveraged but highly liquid. When real estate markets stagnate (as seen in cycles), gold often outperforms, smoothing portfolio volatility.

Understanding Risk Factors: F&O and Market Volatility

For investors using derivatives to hedge their wealth portfolios, the regulatory environment in December 2025 is strict. SEBI's measures to curb retail speculation have altered contract specifications.

1. Bank Nifty Weekly Expiry Discontinued

As of November 2024, Bank Nifty weekly expiry contracts have been discontinued. Traders can now only trade:

  • Nifty 50: Weekly contracts (Every Tuesday).
  • Bank Nifty: Monthly contracts (Last Tuesday of the month).

2. Increased Lot Sizes (Capital Requirement)

To raise the entry barrier, SEBI increased the contract value. As of December 2025:

  • Nifty 50 Lot Size: 75 units (Note: Scheduled to reduce to 65 from Dec 30, 2025).
  • Bank Nifty Lot Size: 35 units (Scheduled to reduce to 30 from Dec 30, 2025).
Risk Implication: The minimum capital required to hedge a portfolio using futures has increased to approximately ₹8-10 lakhs per lot. Investors should use ETFs for hedging unless they have adequate capitalization.

Key Takeaways

  • Tax Regime Matters: For real estate loss set-off benefits (₹2 lakh), you must stick to the Old Tax Regime. The New Regime disallows inter-head set-off.
  • Gold ETF Sweet Spot: Gold ETFs now qualify for 12.5% LTCG after just 12 months, making them the most tax-efficient route for medium-term holding.
  • SGB Scarcity: With no new SGB issues, accumulate via the secondary market, but remember capital gains are taxable on transfer (12.5%).
  • REITs for Yield: For those priced out of physical real estate, REITs offer a tax-efficient alternative, though dividend taxation depends on the SPV structure.
  • F&O Caution: Bank Nifty weekly contracts are gone. Adjust your hedging strategies to monthly cycles or use Nifty 50 weekly options.

FAQs on Wealth Optimization

What is the current LTCG tax rate for Gold ETFs in December 2025?

As per the Finance Act 2025, Gold ETFs are taxed at 12.5% if held for more than 12 months. Gains from units held for less than 12 months are treated as Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG) and taxed at your applicable income tax slab rate.

Can I set off house property loss against salary in the New Tax Regime?

No. In the New Tax Regime for FY 2025-26, you cannot set off loss from house property (interest payment > rental income) against other heads of income like salary. This set-off benefit of up to ₹2 lakh is available only under the Old Tax Regime.

Are Bank Nifty weekly options still available for trading?

No. As per NSE circulars effective November 2024, Bank Nifty weekly expiry contracts have been discontinued. Traders can now only trade Bank Nifty Monthly and Quarterly contracts, which expire on the last Tuesday of the respective period.

What is the lot size for Nifty 50 and Bank Nifty in December 2025?

As of December 2025, the Nifty 50 lot size is 75 units and Bank Nifty is 35 units. These are scheduled to be revised downwards to 65 and 30 units respectively for contracts expiring after December 30, 2025, as per NSE notifications.

Is the 12.5% real estate tax rate mandatory for all properties?

For properties purchased on or after July 23, 2024, the 12.5% rate (without indexation) is mandatory. For properties bought before this date, you have the option to choose between 12.5% (without indexation) or 20% (with indexation), whichever is more beneficial.
